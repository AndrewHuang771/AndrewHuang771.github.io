Background

Currently, only 5% of graduating medical students claim that they are comfortable performing eye and ear exams. Over 50% of cases of acute otitis media are misdiagnosed and the patient is given a treatment they do not need [1]. This results in millions of dollars of wasted resources. The fault lies in the training the medical students receive. Most of the time, they are shown a static image of the ear or eye, with various pathologies and conditions. However, a flat image on a powerpoint slide is very different from looking into an eye or ear through a scope.

Objective

OtoSim exists to bridge the gap between training and real world practice by better simulating the conditions of a real eye or ear exam. By improving the fidelity of medical training, graduating medical students will be more confident in their examinations, producing better results and fewer waste of resources.

Results

I developed a mobile learning platform for the eye called OphthoSim, and aided in the completion of Otosim Mobile, the learning platform for the ear.

For students :

  • How to use an otoscope or ophthalmoscope
  • Testing the student’s accuracy when using an otoscope or ophthalmoscope.
  • Images along with explanatory text of both normal and pathological ears and eyes taken from real patients [2].
  • Animated images for things such as pneumatic otoscopy for the ear, and nystagmus for the eye.
  • Randomized quizzes for practice

For teachers :

  • Make classes with students who are signed up with OtoSim
  • Make online curriculums, presentations, quizzes, and exams for your students
  • View student progress through the modules, quizzes, and exams

Research

Methodology

In order to provide a more realistic rendering of the eye and ear, a few things had to be emphasized: The images must be seen through an otoscope of ophthalmoscope in order to simulate what you would see in practice. The images must be accurate, taken from real patients so that the images realistically represent a variety of pathologies. The images should be animated, in order to simulate a real world scenario where things move around and the patient does not sit still. The best way to achieve these three goals was a simulation system using a phone.

Next Steps

There was a study [3] done in 2009 that showed that training using OtoSim improved medical students’ ability to diagnose conditions related to the ear more so than traditional methods. OphthoSim and the new OtoSim Mobile should also be tested to see if they are more effective than traditional methods.

Conclusion

By creating a more realistic practice environment, medical students will be able to prepare themselves more thoroughly for otological exams in the real world.
